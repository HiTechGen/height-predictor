How do i calculate my future height?


To calculate your future height you need to get your parents height in centimeters then the formula provided below will guide you.


Formula Gene & EF: For Genetic try (40 ≥ 80), For Environmental Factors try (1 ≥ 20 If boy, 20 ≤ 1 If girl)

Formula After Puberty: If you're a boy set Age to (18 ≥ 20), If you're a girl set Age to (15 ≥ 17)


Formula for boy:

(Father)+(Mother)=(Total)

(Total)+(Gene)=(Total)

(Total)÷2=(Total)

(Total)+(Environmental Factors)=(Maximum Height)

(Maximum Height)-(Age)=[Your Future Height]


Formula for girl:

(Father)+(Mother)=(Total)

(Total)+(Gene)=(Total)

(Total)÷2=(Total)

(Total)-(Environmental Factors)=(Maximum Height)

(Maximum Height)-(Age)=[Your Future Height]


Example:

(165cm)+(162cm)=327cm

327cm+60=387cm

387cm÷2=193.5cm

193.5cm+20=213.5cm

213.5cm-18=195.5cm or 196cm [6'4]


This method is not yet scientifically proven so it might give you a wrong result, it's not meant to predict your exact height!


• First of all height is determined by 'Genetic' (from 40% to 80% of your height is gene), if your parents are short then you'll be shorter or if your parents is tall then you'll be taller.

• Second is 'Environmental Factors' (from 1% to 20% of extra height is coming from it), so if you're unhealthy you're unlikely to reach your desired height.


But don't lose hope yet!, There's still a chance for you to grow tall, if you're still in the stage of puberty you still have more time to exercise and grow a few inches taller, To get started your day one exercise try this basic exercise every morning.

If you notice improvements then that it's actually working, but take note: You should always maintain your balance diet and avoid growth stunting factors.
