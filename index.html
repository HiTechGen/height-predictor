How do i calculate my future height?


To calculate your future height you need to get your parents height in centimeters then the formula provided below will guide you.


[ For Gene try 40 ≥ 60, For Environmental Factors try 1 ≥ 20 ]


Formula for boy:

(Father)+(Mother)=(Total)

(Total)+(Gene)=(Total)

(Total)÷2=(Total)

(Total)+(Environmental Factors)=(Maximum Height)

(Maximum Height)-(Age)=[Your Future Height]


Formula for girl:

(Father)+(Mother)=(Total)

(Total)+(Gene)=(Total)

(Total)÷2=(Total)

(Total)-(Environmental Factors)=(Maximum Height)

(Maximum Height)-(Age)=[Your Future Height]


• First of all height is determined by 'Genetic' (from 40% to 60% of your height is gene), if your parents are short you'll be shorter or if your parents is tall you'll be taller.

• Second is 'Environmental Factors' (from 1% to 20% of extra height is coming from it), so if you're unhealthy you're unlikely to reach your desired height.


But don't lose hope yet!, There's still a chance for you to grow tall, if you're still in the stage of puberty you still have more time to exercise and grow a few inches taller, To get started your day one exercise try this basic exercise every morning.

If you notice some improvements then that means it's actually working for you, but take note, you should always maintain your balance diet and avoid growth stunting factors.
